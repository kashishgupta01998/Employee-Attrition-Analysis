# Employee Attrition Analysis using Excel

## Project Overview

Employee attrition is one of the biggest challenges faced by organizations as it directly impacts productivity, recruitment costs, employee morale, and overall business performance. This project analyzes employee attrition data using **Microsoft Excel** to identify the key factors influencing employee turnover and provide actionable insights for HR professionals and business leaders.

The analysis explores employee demographics, job roles, performance, compensation, work experience, and satisfaction levels to uncover trends that can help organizations improve employee retention.

---

## Project Objectives

The primary objectives of this project are to:

- Analyze the overall employee attrition rate.
- Identify departments and job roles with high employee turnover.
- Understand how demographic factors influence attrition.
- Evaluate the impact of job satisfaction, work-life balance, and performance ratings.
- Study compensation and tenure-related trends.
- Develop an interactive Excel dashboard for HR decision-making.
- Provide business recommendations to reduce employee attrition.

## Key Insights

### Department Analysis

- Certain departments experience significantly higher employee turnover than others.

### Job Role Analysis

- Some job roles consistently show higher attrition, indicating potential workload or career growth concerns.

### Age Analysis

- Younger employees tend to leave more frequently than experienced employees.

### Job Satisfaction

- Lower job satisfaction strongly correlates with higher attrition.

### Overtime

- Employees working overtime are more likely to leave the organization.

### Compensation

- Employees in lower salary bands show higher attrition rates.

### Performance

- Monitoring high-performing employees is essential to prevent loss of valuable talent.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

- Improve onboarding programs for new employees.
- Strengthen employee engagement initiatives.
- Review compensation packages for high-risk employee groups.
- Reduce excessive overtime through workload balancing.
- Provide career development and promotion opportunities.
- Conduct regular employee satisfaction surveys.
- Design retention strategies for departments with high attrition.
